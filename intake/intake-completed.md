- Writing voice profile:
  Write like a trusted local plumbing expert who is friendly, practical, and easy to
  understand. The tone should feel warm, confident, and straightforward, like a
  reliable plumber explaining the problem clearly to a homeowner.

  Use "we" throughout to make the business feel personal and local. Speak directly to
  the customer using "you" and "your home" so the copy feels helpful and specific.

  Keep the writing simple, clear, and reassuring. Focus on solving real customer
  problems like leaks, clogs, water heater issues, sewer backups, fixture repairs,
  emergency plumbing, sump pump issues, and general plumbing stress.

  Sound professional without sounding corporate. Pete's Plumbing should feel like a
  dependable small business with real experience, not a national franchise or a pushy
  sales company.

  Emphasize trust, speed, honesty, cleanliness, clear communication, and quality
  workmanship. Mention licensed and insured service, upfront pricing, same-day
  availability, respectful technicians, and clear explanations where it fits naturally.

  Avoid corporate-speak, exaggerated claims, scare tactics, excessive statistics,
  technical jargon, overly clever headlines, and anything that sounds like a generic
  brochure. Avoid phrases like "industry-leading solutions," "premier provider,"
  "world-class service," "cutting-edge," and "best-in-class."

  Use short paragraphs, clear headings, and scannable sections. One idea per
  paragraph. Prioritize clarity over creativity.
- Target audience:
  Homeowners and small property owners in suburban Armonk and nearby Westchester
  County communities, typically ages 30–65, who need a reliable plumber they can trust
  in their home. They value professionalism, honest communication, clean work, and fast
  response times more than finding the cheapest option.
